## Hypothesis

IF we conduct 30+ structured interviews with university students about the GP opportunity (no product shown), THEN we'll identify motivations, concerns, and language that differentiate the student persona from order bookers, BECAUSE students have distinct career-stage motivations that current GP messaging doesn't address.

## Success Criteria

- **Validated if:** 30+ interviews completed AND clear persona patterns emerge (3+ distinct themes vs order booker persona) AND at least one actionable messaging direction identified for follow-up framing test
- **Invalidated if:** students show no meaningful interest in the opportunity regardless of how it's framed in conversation — the persona is not viable for GP recruitment
- **Inconclusive if:** <20 interviews completed OR responses too heterogeneous to identify patterns

## The Experiment

**Type:** Research

Zero structured data existed on the university student persona as a potential growth partner. This research establishes baseline interest, identifies motivations and deal-breakers, and directly informs messaging for a follow-up recruitment experiment.

## Minimum Viable Test

- **Intervention:** Structured recorded interviews (10-15 min each) with university students about the GP opportunity. No product shown. Pure persona research.
- **Who:** 60 university students across 6 universities in Karachi. Mix of years (1st through final) and disciplines (BBA, CS, ACF, Social Sciences, Economics, Humanities).
- **How:** In-person recorded interviews using structured question protocol via Google Form. 13 interviews also recorded as voice memos.
- **Duration:** 3 days (March 2-4, 2026)
- **Interviewers:** 13 coordinators, led by Ahmed Abdul Rauf (48% of interviews) and Alveena Khan (17%)

### Three Sub-Personas

**The Hustler (~15-20%)** — Entrepreneurial, already earning, sees this as one more income stream. Wants uncapped earnings, leaderboards, referral commissions. Minimum: PKR 40-60K. "40 shops in two weeks is easy if you have the strategy."

**The Cautious Pragmatist (~50-55%)** — Interested but risk-averse. Needs guaranteed base + clear expectations before committing. Biggest fear: working 5 hours and earning zero. Minimum: PKR 30-40K. "Pure commission is scary because I might work for 5 hours and earn zero."

**The Reluctant Observer (~10-15%)** — Academically focused, sees field sales as beneath them. Would only consider with internship/research framing. Fixed salary only. "Why doing it after graduating from renowned university."

## Course Corrections (from mid-flight review)

**Critical flags:**
1. **48% single-interviewer concentration** — Ahmed Abdul Rauf conducted 29/60 interviews with zero recordings. No way to verify technique or data quality for nearly half the sample.
2. **82% friend/referral sampling bias** — Coordinators recruited from personal networks, not random intercepts. Responses skew toward people already predisposed to gig work.
3. **Form vs recording gaps** — Mahrukh's form entry says "Nothing" for concerns, but her 4,900-char recording reveals deep reservations about shopkeeper rejection and app credibility.
4. **Zero probing across all 13 recordings** — Interviewers accept first answers without follow-up. "Acha" → next question pattern throughout.

**Corrective measures for remaining interviews:**
- Require voice recording for all interviews (not just 13/60)
- Cap any single interviewer at 20% of total sample
- Add random-intercept quota (minimum 40% non-referral)
- Train interviewers on probing technique: "Tell me more about that" after every key response
- Cross-check form entries against recordings for quality audit

## What Happens Next

- **Immediate:** Implement course corrections above for any additional interviews
- **If validated** → Design a framing A/B test using the top 2-3 messaging themes:
  - **Theme A:** "Base + bonus side hustle" (targets Cautious Pragmatists, 50-55% of pool)
  - **Theme B:** "Uncapped earnings, be your own boss" (targets Hustlers, 15-20%)
  - **Theme C:** "Field research internship with certificate" (targets Reluctant Observers, 10-15%)
- **First cohort recruitment:** Use earnings proof from existing GPs as social proof. 47% say they'd refer friends only after seeing real money.
- **Training must include:** Rejection handling scripts (27% quit risk), app reliability walkthrough (10% quit risk), clear pay calculator showing realistic weekly earnings.
