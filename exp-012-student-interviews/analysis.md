Half want a hybrid model. Only 25% insist on pure fixed salary. The remaining 23% actually prefer pure performance pay.

## Persona Synthesis

Three distinct sub-personas emerge from the data:

### Persona 1: The Hustler

**Archetype:** Entrepreneurial, already earning, sees this as one more income stream.
**Prevalence:** ~15–20% of sample (roughly the "High interest + Easy/Doable + Performance-based" overlap)

- **Demographics:** Typically 20–22, male, BBA or Marketing, prior gig experience (dropshipping, social media, freelance)
- **Key motivations:** Money first, resume value second. Wants to earn as much as possible with no cap. Sees this as a "project I am running," not a job.
- **Key concerns:** Earnings cap, slow payments, app reliability. Will not sell a product they don't believe in.
- **Pay preference:** Mix or pure per-merchant. Actively wants performance-based pay. Minimum expectation: PKR 40–60K.
- **Messaging that resonates:** "Uncapped earnings," "be your own boss," "top partners earn X," leaderboards, referral override commissions.
- **Quotes:** "40 shops in two weeks is easy if you have the strategy." / "In business, effort should decide income."
- **Representative respondents:** Ammar, Muzammil, Muhammad (affiliate marketer), Ali ("project I am running"), Haider

### Persona 2: The Cautious Pragmatist

**Archetype:** Interested but risk-averse. Needs certainty before committing.
**Prevalence:** ~50–55% of sample (the "Medium interest + Maybe + Doable/Hard" bulk)

- **Demographics:** 20–22, male or female, BBA or ACF, some have prior gig experience but nothing intense
- **Key motivations:** Extra income alongside studies. Values flexibility and schedule control. Wants to know exactly what they're getting into before starting.
- **Key concerns:** Unrealistic targets, rude shopkeepers, delayed/unclear pay, time conflicts with exams. The biggest barrier is uncertainty — "what if I work 5 hours and earn zero?"
- **Pay preference:** Mix (base + bonus). Needs a guaranteed base to feel safe. Minimum expectation: PKR 30–40K.
- **Messaging that resonates:** "Guaranteed base + bonus for performance," "flexible around your class schedule," "average partner earns X in their first week," testimonials from other students.
- **Quotes:** "Pure commission is scary because I might work for 5 hours and earn zero." / "Transparency about earning and expectations is very important."
- **Representative respondents:** Sushil, Muhammad ("base covers the basic effort"), Hasan, Mohammad, Bakhtawar, Javeria

### Persona 3: The Reluctant Observer

**Archetype:** Academically focused, sees field sales as beneath them or outside their comfort zone.
**Prevalence:** ~10–15% of sample (the "Low interest + Unlikely + Hard/No way" cluster)

- **Demographics:** Mixed ages, often CS or social sciences, may have no prior gig experience
- **Key motivations:** Primarily academic achievement. Would only consider this if it were framed as an internship or had clear career value.
- **Key concerns:** Dignity ("why doing it after graduating from renowned university"), physical effort, social awkwardness with shopkeepers.
- **Pay preference:** Fixed salary (if they'd do it at all). Not interested in performance risk.
- **Messaging that resonates:** Very little in current positioning. Could be reached with: "field research internship" framing, university credit, certificate of completion, or "market research" rather than "sales."
- **Quotes:** "Why doing it after graduating from renowned university" / "It's outside my league"
- **Representative respondents:** Mohib, Zeeshan, Nafeah, Mustafa (self-described "lazy"), Muntazir

## Key Findings

1. **The interest pool is large but conditionally committed.** 90% show Medium or High interest, and 30% say they "Would join." But 62% are in "Maybe" — their conversion depends entirely on first-week experience design: clear pay, manageable targets, and no ugly surprises.

2. **PKR 30–50K/month is the magic range.** The median expectation is PKR 40K, and 51% of respondents would accept PKR 40K or less. A compensation structure targeting PKR 35–45K total (base + performance) for a half-time commitment would satisfy the majority without overshooting.

3. **Performance-based pay is surprisingly popular — when a base exists.** 62% accept "lower base + more per sign-up" and only 17% insist on a guaranteed salary. But "pure commission" triggers fear. The winning structure: a meaningful base (say PKR 15–20K) plus per-merchant bonuses that make the total realistic at PKR 35–45K.

4. **Shopkeeper rejection is the #1 quit risk.** 27% cite negative shopkeeper responses as their quit trigger — above even pay issues (18%) and unrealistic targets (22%). Training must include rejection handling, scripts for common objections, and mental framing ("9 out of 10 say no — that's normal").

5. **The app is your credibility proxy.** Multiple respondents said they would quit if the app is buggy or makes them look bad. Students are lending their personal reputation to the product. If the onboarding flow crashes or confuses a shopkeeper, you lose the student — not just the merchant.

6. **Referral is driven by proof of earnings, not promises.** 47% of referral motivators boil down to "show me the money first." Students want to see real paychecks hitting real accounts. The most effective recruitment strategy is not marketing — it's paying the first cohort well and on time, then letting them recruit their friends.

7. **"Side hustle" is the dominant mental model, not "job."** 50% explicitly call it a side hustle. Marketing it as a "full-time opportunity" or "career" will alienate the majority. Lean into: flexibility, own-your-schedule, extra cash, experience that looks good on a CV. The IBA sub-segment responds well to "internship" and "field experience" framing for resume value.
