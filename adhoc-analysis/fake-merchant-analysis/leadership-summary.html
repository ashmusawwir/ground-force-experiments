104 Merchants in scope (Feb 1+)

We cannot reliably detect fake merchant enrollments with data alone. Most fraud signals that appear suspicious turn out to describe normal fleet behavior.

One field ambassador enrolled 10 merchants across a 12-day period, submitted the majority outside standard field hours (before 9 am or after 7 pm PKT), and logged 11 field visits — converting 10 of 11 visits into enrollments (91% rate vs ~9% fleet average). Four of those merchants were confirmed fake by the verification team.

What Actually Stops This

Recommendation Verification-gated incentives

Call or WhatsApp every newly enrolled merchant within 3 days: "Did a ZAR ambassador demonstrate the app to you?" This single step is the verification gate.

Make part of the ambassador's commission conditional on that verification passing. The exact split depends on the ghost rate — if the ghost rate is low, an immediate partial payment is fine; if it's high, hold the full payment until verified. Either way, a fake merchant can never confirm a real visit. The incentive to create fake enrollments disappears.

Supporting evidence: day-3 merchant callback programs have been used to surface 12–18% ghost-enrollment rates in comparable field-agent programs. At ZAR's current volume, the ops cost of calling ~50–100 new merchants per week is trivial relative to the fraud exposure. No app changes required — phone and WhatsApp only.
