4 Systemic Fix

What needs to change to prevent this class of incident from recurring

Problem Fix
Detection gap — no alerting when a merchant's app breaks. ZAR only finds out when the merchant escalates through a contractor. There is no passive signal (session heartbeat, last-seen timestamp, app error report) that surfaces a stuck or broken merchant app. By the time the team hears about it, the merchant has already lost a transaction and started composing a voice note. Automated health monitoring for active merchants — last app open, last successful transaction, error rate. Flag any active merchant who has not opened their app in 72h+ or who has had a failed transaction attempt. Reactive complaint → proactive outreach.
Escalation path missing — contractors have no structured way to report a broken merchant app. The only option was an informal WhatsApp message, relayed through a 4-hop chain (merchant → ambassador → ground force → devs) that destroys diagnostic context at every step. No ticket was created, no SLA was set, and the issue was still unresolved 13 hours later. One-tap "merchant app broken" report from the contractor-facing tool → auto-creates a support ticket with merchant ID, timestamp, and contractor info pre-filled → merchant receives an acknowledgment within 1 hour. No relayed WhatsApp chain, no context loss.
Manual workaround is not a solution. Facilitating an out-of-app $1,400 trade via 50 WhatsApp messages and 2 phone calls at 11:30 PM is a people-powered patch. It is not repeatable, not documentable, and creates dependency on specific individuals being reachable at night. Document the fallback procedure for app-down scenarios (who to call, what to communicate to the merchant). Hard limit: if manual workaround is used more than once for the same merchant in a 30-day window, the app issue is force-escalated to P1.
No priority support tier for high-value merchants. A merchant with 61 transactions, $37,902 sold, and 29 users onboarded is in the top tier of the network. Their app breaking should not enter the same queue as a day-1 onboarding issue. Define an "Active Merchant" SLA tier (≥20 transactions OR ≥$10K sold OR ≥10 users onboarded). Any app issue from this tier is auto-tagged P1 with a 4h resolution target and a direct phone acknowledgment within 1h.
